Edmonton Housing Market Overview: 2025

Edmonton's real estate market has experienced robust growth in 2025, with home prices reaching new highs. The city remains one of Canada's most affordable large urban centers, yet it's not immune to the national trend of rising property values.

Average Home Prices

  • Average home price (March 2025): $460,685

  • Benchmark home price: $438,100 (up 12% year-over-year)

  • Median home price: $445,000

These figures mark all-time highs for Edmonton, reflecting a competitive and active market environment.

Edmonton House Values by Property Type

Detached Homes

  • Average price: $574,872 (up 11% year-over-year)

  • Benchmark price: $516,400 (up 14% year-over-year)

  • Median sale price: $535,000

Detached homes continue to be in high demand, driving substantial price growth.

Semi-Detached Homes

  • Average price: $430,646 (up 5.4% year-over-year)

Townhouses

  • Average price: $314,791 (up 11% year-over-year)

  • Benchmark price: $303,900 (up 20% year-over-year)

Townhouses have seen some of the fastest price increases, making them a hot segment in 2025.

Condos/Apartments

  • Average price: $217,701 (up 12% year-over-year)

  • Benchmark price: $215,400 (up 15% year-over-year)

Condos remain the most affordable entry point into Edmonton's housing market, but they're also experiencing notable price gains.

What's Driving Edmonton House Values in 2025?

Several factors are influencing Edmonton house values this year:

  • Low inventory: Listings remain tight, with inventory down 13.4% year-over-year.

  • Strong demand: Early spring 2025 saw a surge in activity, with a 14.3% increase in unit sales from January to February.

  • Economic growth: Renewed economic activity and job growth are boosting buyer confidence.

  • Interest rates: Mortgage rates remain relatively low, encouraging more buyers to enter the market.

  • Seller's market: The sales-to-new-listings ratio is at 66%, indicating strong competition among buyers.

Key Takeaways
